The Efficiency Gap

The "Static" Trap

Traditional CNNs are computationally rigid. They process a simple image of a clear sky with the exact same FLOPs count as a complex urban scene.

  • Battery Drain: Massive energy waste on IoT sensors.
  • Latency: Unacceptable delays for real-time autonomous navigation.
  • Over-parameterization: >90% of weights in VGG networks are often redundant.
Ontario Tech University 3 / 13

Existing Approaches

Static Pruning

Method: Permanently removing weights based on magnitude magnitude.

Limitation: Destructive. Once a channel is gone, it cannot be recovered for "hard" samples. Requires complex iterative fine-tuning.

Mixture of Experts

Method: Routing inputs to specific expert blocks.

Limitation: Introduces dynamic control flow which causes thread divergence on GPUs, often leading to slower wall-clock time despite fewer FLOPs.

Ontario Tech University 4 / 13

Proposed: Latency-Aware Gating

We propose LAG: An end-to-end differentiable mechanism that learns to turn channels ON or OFF based on the input feature map itself.

Input Adaptive

Easy images use 20% capacity. Hard images use 100%.

Differentiable

Uses "Soft Gating" during training to allow gradient flow.

Hardware Ready

Uses "Hard Gating" during inference for real speedup.

Mathematical Derivation

The Relaxed Gate

To enable backpropagation through binary decisions, we use a temperature-scaled sigmoid:

$$ \sigma_T(x) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-x/T}} $$

Where $T$ is the temperature. As $T \to 0$, the function approaches a step function (Hard Gating).

The Objective Function

We minimize a joint loss:

$$ L_{total} = L_{CE} + \lambda \sum_{l=1}^{L} ||g_l||_1 $$

The first term $L_{CE}$ maintains accuracy. The second term induces sparsity (L1 regularization on gates).

Results II: ResNet-18 on CIFAR-10

The Lottery Ticket Hypothesis

For ResNet-18, LAG successfully identified a sub-network with 40% fewer FLOPs that matched the baseline accuracy exactly.

This confirms that ResNet contains "winning tickets" — sparse sub-topologies capable of full performance — and LAG finds them automatically in a single training run.

Future Work

Expand performance evaluation

We are investigating the limitations and capabilities of this technique, including extending the optimization beyond parameter counts toward integrating real FPGA cycle measurements and mobile GPU latency into the loss function.

LLMs & Transformers

Applying the LAG framework to Vision Transformers (ViT) and Large Language Models (GPT) to dynamically prune tokens, reducing the cost of generation.

Dynamic sparsity is key to next-generation Edge AI.
